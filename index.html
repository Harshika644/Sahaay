How Local NGOs Saved Lives in Assam Floods

In July 2025, massive floods struck Assam, affecting thousands. Local NGOs stepped in when access to remote villages was blocked. With the help of Sahaay's real-time map, volunteers coordinated relief across affected districts, dropping supplies by boat and drone. Stories of rescue, like the one in Barpeta where volunteers carried elders across waist-high water, remind us of the importance of quick action and technology-driven support systems.

×

Using Sahaay’s Map Feature for Relief

Sahaay’s interactive Live Aid Map enabled disaster teams to view shelters, aid points, and flooded zones in real time. During the Bihar monsoon season, volunteers could pinpoint isolated communities and send tailored support, reducing redundancy and speeding up help delivery. This feature has become a critical tool for NGO coordination and rapid-response planning across India.

Voices of Hope: Stories from Survivors

“Thanks to Sahaay, my family got food and medicine within a day of the landslide,” says Reema, a mother of two in Himachal. Many such testimonials show how the platform connected isolated families to NGOs nearby. Real-time chatbot assistance helped people navigate help services, and the NGO directory allowed survivors to find help even without knowing local organizations. Sahaay became their digital friend in a time of need.
